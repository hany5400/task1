The Mona Lisa, painted by Leonardo da Vinci between 1503 and 1506, is one of the most iconic works of art in history. Housed in the Louvre Museum in Paris, this Renaissance masterpiece is renowned for its enigmatic expression and masterful use of sfumato, a technique that creates a soft transition between colors and tones. The subject, believed to be Lisa Gherardini, the wife of a Florentine merchant, is portrayed with a mysterious smile that has intrigued viewers for centuries.
